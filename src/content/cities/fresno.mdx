## How to Choose a Legal Nurse Consultant in Fresno

- **Verify credentials before anything else.** LNCC (from AALNC) and CLNC (Vickie Milazzo Institute) are the two recognized marks. Either is legitimate; neither is guaranteed without checking the issuing body's registry. California has no state licensure for LNC work, so the credential is the only quality signal you've got.
- **Match specialty to case type.** An LNC who spent 20 years in an ICU is the wrong pick for a workers' comp soft-tissue case. Fresno's case mix skews toward agricultural injuries, vehicle accidents on Highway 99, and hospital negligence at CRMC and Saint Agnes — find someone whose clinical background maps to your facts.
- **Ask specifically about California standard-of-care familiarity.** CDPH regulations, CMS conditions of participation, and California nursing scope of practice have meaningful differences from other states. An LNC licensed and practicing in California is a different asset than one transplanted from Texas.
- **Request a sample work product.** A medical chronology or case summary from a prior engagement (redacted) tells you more about their output quality than any resume. If they hesitate, that's information too.
- **Confirm availability and turnaround before you engage.** Fresno's plaintiff bar is small enough that the best LNCs get booked deep, especially during ag-litigation season. Don't assume a credentialed consultant is also an available one.

> **Pro Tip:** For life care plan cases, look specifically for CNLCP or MSCC credentials — general LNCC certification doesn't mean the consultant has done actuarial-style damages quantification. That's a different skill set, and Fresno courts have seen enough sloppy life care plans that judges notice.

## What to Expect

LNC engagements in Fresno typically run $2,000–$10,000 per case depending on complexity, record volume, and whether testimony is involved. A focused medical record review for a single-defendant personal injury matter sits toward the low end; a multi-defendant medical malpractice case with expert witness coordination and trial prep support will push the ceiling. Expect an initial records review turnaround of 5–10 business days for a standard case volume.

> **Reality Check:** The most common billing mistake is treating the initial case review as the full engagement cost. It isn't. If the case proceeds to discovery, expert witness screening, deposition prep, and trial support each layer on additional fees. Get a scope-of-work agreement that distinguishes the review phase from litigation support phases — or you'll be surprised by invoice two.
