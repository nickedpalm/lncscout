## How to Choose a Legal Nurse Consultant in Mesa

- **Verify credentials before anything else.** LNCC (from AALNC) and CLNC (from the Vickie Milazzo Institute) are the two designations that matter. LNCC requires 2,000 documented hours of LNC experience plus a proctored exam — it's not a weekend course. If someone can't produce their certification number on request, move on.
- **Match specialty to case type.** A nurse with 20 years in orthopedics can tear apart a spinal fusion complication. She probably can't tell you where a labor and delivery team deviated from the standard of care. Be explicit about the clinical area before your first call.
- **Ask for a sample work product.** Any experienced LNC should be able to show you a redacted medical chronology or causation analysis. Hedging on this is your answer.
- **Confirm Arizona-specific familiarity.** Arizona's pure comparative fault rules and AHCCCS lien recovery requirements add wrinkles that out-of-state LNCs routinely miss. Ask directly whether they've worked cases under Arizona law and in Maricopa County courts.
- **Define litigation support scope upfront.** Some LNCs do records review only. Others prepare expert witness lists, draft interrogatory responses, and attend depositions as second-chair support. Know what you need before the engagement letter is signed.

> **Pro Tip:** For catastrophic injury or long-term disability damages cases, look specifically for CNLCP (Certified Nurse Life Care Planner) or MSCC (Medicare Set-Aside Certified Consultant) credentials. General LNC experience doesn't translate directly into defensible life care plans or Medicare set-aside allocations — those require a separate, specialized skill set.

## What to Expect

Most Mesa engagements run $2,000–$10,000 depending on record volume, clinical complexity, and whether you need expert witness sourcing or just a chronology and causation memo. Hourly rates for records review typically land at $150–$250/hour; full litigation support packages are often quoted flat. Standard records review turnaround is 5–10 business days, though multi-provider files with gaps in records can run longer.

> **Reality Check:** The most common scoping mistake is budgeting only for the initial review. A 1,200-page hospital record that looks clean at intake can expand fast once the LNC flags missing providers, identifies the need for a literature search, or discovers the records are incomplete. Get a not-to-exceed estimate before engagement starts — or build a formal change-order clause into the agreement.
