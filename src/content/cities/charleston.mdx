## How to Choose a Legal Nurse Consultant in Charleston

- **Verify the credential tier, not just the credential.** LNCC (from AALNC) requires 2,000 hours of LNC experience plus a written exam — it's the gold standard. CLNC is a training certificate from Vickie Milazzo Institute, which is a legitimate starting point but not the same thing. Know the difference before your first call.
- **Match specialty to case type.** Charleston's legal market skews toward maritime injury (thanks to the port), workers' comp from the manufacturing corridor along I-26, and medical malpractice at MUSC. An LNC with 15 years of oncology nursing reviewing a crane collapse case is the wrong tool for the job.
- **Ask about South Carolina statute of limitations familiarity.** SC has a 3-year SOL for medical malpractice with specific notice requirements (§ 15-79-125). An LNC who routinely works SC cases will flag timeline issues in the records review that an out-of-state practitioner might miss entirely.
- **Request a sample work product.** Any experienced LNC should be able to show you a redacted chronology or case summary. If they hesitate, that's your answer.
- **Confirm courtroom availability upfront.** If you may need a testifying expert or a certified life care planner for damages, clarify whether the LNC holds CNLCP or MSCC credentials — those aren't standard, and retrofitting a case with a separate expert late in litigation is expensive.

> **Pro Tip:** Charleston's federal docket (D.S.C.) moves fast — cases in the Eastern Division can hit trial within 18 months of filing. Hire an LNC who quotes turnaround in days, not weeks.

## What to Expect

Most LNC engagements in Charleston run $2,000–$10,000 per case, with straightforward records review on the lower end and full litigation support packages — initial case screening, chronology, standard of care analysis, expert witness identification, and deposition prep — pushing toward the top. Hourly rates typically land between $150–$300/hr depending on specialty and credential level, with flat-fee initial screenings offered by some practitioners for early case evaluation.

> **Reality Check:** The most common mistake attorneys make is hiring on hourly rate alone. A cheaper LNC who takes 40 hours to produce a chronology that an experienced practitioner delivers in 12 hours isn't actually cheaper — it's just a slower invoice. Ask for case volume and average turnaround data, not just the rate.

## Local Market Overview

Charleston sits at a useful intersection for LNC work: MUSC Health is one of the Southeast's major academic medical centers, generating both complex malpractice exposure and a deep local pool of nurses with subspecialty clinical backgrounds. The port, the growing tech and aerospace presence, and a steady stream of tourism-related personal injury cases mean demand for LNC services here is both broad and specialized — which is why the right match between your case type and a consultant's clinical background matters more in this market than in a smaller metro.
