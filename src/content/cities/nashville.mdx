## How to Choose a Legal Nurse Consultant in Nashville

- **Verify the credential, not just the title.** "Legal nurse consultant" isn't a protected term — anyone can use it. Look for LNCC (board-certified through AALNC) or CLNC (Vickie Milazzo Institute). If the case involves life care planning, add CNLCP to that checklist. In Tennessee, credentialed LNCs are the standard on any case that goes to trial.
- **Match clinical background to the case type.** A former ICU nurse is the wrong choice for a long-term-care neglect case. Nashville has deep talent pools in trauma, cardiac, and oncology nursing thanks to Vanderbilt, Saint Thomas, and TriStar — use that specificity to your advantage.
- **Ask for a sample work product before you retain.** A credible LNC will share a redacted chronology or causation analysis without hesitation. If they can't produce an example, that tells you something.
- **Confirm Tennessee-specific regulatory familiarity.** Tennessee's medical malpractice statute (T.C.A. § 29-26-115) requires a certificate of good faith from a qualified expert before filing. An LNC who knows this landscape can pre-screen expert witnesses before you've spent a dollar on expert fees.
- **Check their litigation support depth.** Some LNCs cap out at record review. For high-value cases, you want someone who can develop trial exhibits, prep witnesses on medical terminology, and hold up under deposition if designated as a testifying expert.

> **Pro Tip:** Ask specifically whether the LNC has worked with Tennessee's comparative fault framework. Cases involving multiple defendants — common in construction and industrial injury matters in Nashville's booming development sector — require someone who understands how medical causation arguments interact with apportionment.

## What to Expect

Most LNC engagements in Nashville run $2,000–$10,000 depending on case complexity, record volume, and whether testifying expert work is involved — expect the higher end for multi-defendant medical malpractice or life care plan development. Turnaround on a standard records review and chronology is typically two to four weeks; rush work is available but will cost you a premium, and the good consultants book out.

> **Reality Check:** The biggest pricing mistake attorneys make is hiring on hourly rate alone. A $90/hour LNC who takes 80 hours to produce a disorganized chronology costs more — and does more damage — than a $150/hour specialist who delivers a clean, court-ready analysis in 30 hours. Scope and output quality matter more than the rate.
