## How to Choose a Legal Nurse Consultant in Des Moines

- **Credential first, experience second.** An LNCC (from AALNC) or CLNC (Vickie Milazzo Institute) designation isn't just alphabet soup — it means the consultant has passed a standardized examination on legal process and professional standards. In Iowa, where there's no state licensure for LNC work, these credentials are the only external check on quality.
- **Match clinical specialty to case type.** A consultant who spent 20 years in cardiac ICU is the wrong hire for a complex orthopedic liability case. Ask specifically: what units or specialties, what types of records reviewed, and whether they've worked similar case types before.
- **Clarify testifying vs. consulting-only.** Most LNCs in Des Moines work behind the scenes — reviewing records, drafting chronologies, locating expert witnesses. If you need a testifying expert or a certified life care plan (CNLCP), say so upfront. Not all are qualified or willing to step into that role.
- **Ask about Iowa court familiarity.** Iowa's medical malpractice landscape has specific procedural wrinkles — mandatory notice periods, certificate of merit requirements. A consultant who primarily works federal or out-of-state cases may not be current on Iowa District Court norms.
- **Get a sample work product.** A redacted medical record chronology or a causation analysis summary tells you more about fit in ten minutes than any phone screen.

> **Pro Tip:** Iowa is home to major insurance carriers including Principal Financial, Nationwide, and EMC Insurance — all headquartered in Des Moines. LNCs with claims-review backgrounds are more common here than in markets without that insurer concentration, which can be an asset for coverage work but a gap for plaintiff-side litigation support. Ask explicitly which side of the table they've worked most.

## What to Expect

LNC engagements in Des Moines typically run $2,000–$10,000 per case, with hourly rates in the $150–$300 range depending on credentials, specialty, and scope of work. A standard records review and case summary for a single-plaintiff PI matter sits at the lower end; complex multi-party medical malpractice with expert witness screening and deposition preparation runs higher. Most consultants deliver an initial case assessment within 5–10 business days of receiving records.

> **Reality Check:** The most common pricing mistake is budgeting for records review only, then getting surprised by the cost of follow-on work — deposition prep, trial support, or a life care plan can each rival the original engagement fee. Get a scope-of-work estimate that covers likely downstream tasks, not just the intake phase.

## Local Market Overview

Des Moines punches above its weight for legal volume — it's the state capital, the seat of the Iowa Supreme Court, and home to a dense concentration of insurance company headquarters, which generates steady demand for LNC services across both litigation and claims functions. The Polk County courts handle a disproportionate share of Iowa's complex civil litigation, and with UnityPoint Health and MercyOne running major regional hospital systems out of the city, medical record complexity in local cases reflects a full-spectrum tertiary care environment.
